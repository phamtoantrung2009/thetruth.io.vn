## 3. The 3 Core Tensions

### Income vs Assets

You earn well. You save responsibly. You a  still can't afford70m² apartment in Hanoi or Ho Chi Minh City.

**Mechanism:** Salary builds income, not ownership. Asset prices inflation runs at 12-15%/year. Wage growth runs at 4-6%/year. The gap is deliberate — not a bug, a feature.

The math was designed for capital accumulation, not labor accumulation. Your income is your trap.

---

### Salary vs Inflation

Your parents bought an apartment in 1995 for 50 million đồng. You earn 10x that monthly and can't afford one today.

**Mechanism:** Nominal wages increased. Real purchasing power decreased. When you "save" in a bank, you lose 4-5% annually to inflation. When you "save" in real estate, you ride an asset bubble you can't join.

The minimum wage in 2025 is 4.9 million đồng. The average apartment is 55 million đồng/m². The ladder was removed while you were told to climb.

---

### Hard Work vs Capital

You work 50-60 hours/week. You did everything right — education, career, discipline. Someone with half your effort and family connections is three steps ahead.

**Mechanism:** In Vietnam's economy, capital > labor. Access to capital (inheritance, connections, property ownership) compounds. Labor effort is linear. The return on effort is negative once fixed costs (housing, family duty) are factored in.

Working harder won't close the gap. Understanding the mechanism might.
